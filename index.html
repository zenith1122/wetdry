Dry Season — Wurrgeng & Gurrung
May – October · Currently active
⌄

The most popular time to explore Darwin's nature. Clear skies, low humidity and comfortable temperatures make outdoor wildlife spotting ideal. Waterholes draw animals in large concentrations as water sources dry up across the landscape.

🐦 Migratory birds 🐊 Saltwater crocs 🦘 Wallabies 🦎 Goannas 🦇 Flying foxes 🐍 Brown tree snake
⚡
Build-Up — Gunumeleng
October – December · Approaching
⌄

Hot, humid and electric. The build-up brings dramatic afternoon storms, spectacular lightning shows over Darwin Harbour, and the return of wildlife from drier inland areas. Temperatures climb to 35°C+ with humidity above 80%.

🦋 Butterflies arrive 🐸 Frogs emerge ⚡ Storm activity 🌸 Wildflowers 🦜 Parrots flock
🌧️
Wet Season — Gudjewg
December – March · Spectacular but challenging
⌄

Darwin transforms. Monsoon rains flood rivers and plains, filling Kakadu's waterfalls and bringing an explosion of tropical vegetation. Wildlife disperses across the landscape. Waterfalls like Wangi and Gunlom reach peak flow.

🌊 Waterfalls peak 🐊 Croc nesting 🐟 Barramundi breed 🌿 Lush vegetation 🦆 Magpie geese 🐛 Insects explode
🌀
Knock 'em Down — Bangkerreng
March – May · Season transition
⌄

Named for the storms that flatten the tall spear grass. Violent gusts level the landscape, making way for the dry season. A dramatic time to witness nature's reset — flooded roads begin to recede and the first dry breezes arrive from the south.

🌾 Spear grass falls 🐦 Bird congregation 🌅 Dramatic sunsets 🐊 Crocs disperse

Fogg Dam Conservation Reserve
One of Australia's best birdwatching sites. Magpie geese, brolgas, and jabiru congregate around the dam walls. 40km east of Darwin.
🐦 Birdwatching ☀️ Best: Dry
🐊
🐊 CC ↗
Adelaide River Jumping Crocs
Home to large saltwater crocodiles. Jumping croc cruises run daily. Best in dry season when crocs congregate near banks.
🐊 Crocodiles ☀️ Best: Dry
🦇
🦇 CC ↗
Darwin Botanic Gardens
Home to Australia's largest urban flying fox colony. Every evening thousands depart at sunset in a dramatic display.
🦇 Flying foxes 🌅 Sunset
🌊
🌊 CC ↗
Litchfield National Park
Magnetic termite mounds, waterfalls and swimming holes. Wangi Falls peak during and just after wet season.
🌊 Waterfalls 🌧️ Best: Wet
🦅
🐊 CC ↗
Kakadu National Park
UNESCO World Heritage site. Over 280 bird species, saltwater and freshwater crocs, and ancient rock art. 170km east of Darwin.
🦅 Wildlife ☀️ Accessible: Dry

🦅 CC ↗
Mary River Wetlands
Incredible density of saltwater crocodiles — one of the highest in the world. Famous barramundi fishing in dry season.
